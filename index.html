About me

I am working as a student project employee at IGTEs Aero- and Vibroacoustics group. Here I am investigating both noise generation mechanisms in automotive applications, as well as employing Reduced Order Modeling (ROM) techniques for aeroacoustic flows.

I am doing my masters degree in an indiviual degree program in Computational Engineering at the Graz University of Technology, the curriculum can be found here. It is composed of theoretical and computational fluid mechanics, acoustics, nonlinear solid mechanics, electromagnetics, multiphysics, optimization and nonlinear control systems.

Currently I work on my masters thesis, which is about fluid-structure-acoustic-interactions (FSAI) in aeroacoustics. Where I am conducting high-fidelity CFD simulations using MGLET on the Vienna Scientific Cluster (VSC).

href="https://km-turbulenz.de/products-services// Before that I aquired a bachelors degree in Physics. In my bachelors thesis I worked on nanofluidics. More exactly on water transport through flexible carbon nanotubes, where I conducted molecular dynamics and finite element simulations to check the applicability of continuum mechanics on the nanoscale. The work was done at the Institute of Theoretical and Computational Physics at the Graz University of Technology. My thesis can be found here and the final presentation can be found here.
